Definition

  • Concision is brevity relative to purpose

Do Not Waste the Main Elements of the Sentence

WORDY The fact of the war had the effect of causing many changes.

CONCISE The war caused many changes.


The core idea should be conveyed by subject, verb, and object

  • Fact had effect v.s. War caused changes

Awkward Anticipatory Construction

WORDY This is the kind of golfer that is called a hacker.

CONCISE This kind of golfer is called a hacker.


Whether idiom or emphasis justifies it

  • “It is true that we did not like the idea at first” to “That we did not like the idea at first is true”
    • saves one word but results in a stiff sentence, too formal for many occasions.
  • “This is the man who witnessed the accident” to “This man witnessed the accident”
    • deemphasizes the point, hardly an improvement if the writer wants to make a strong statement.

Express Modifiers in the Fewest Possible Words

  • Using adverbs

WORDY He acted in an unnatural way

CONCISE He acted unnaturally.


  • Indirectly connected

WORDY She prefers wines having a French origin

CONCISE She prefers French wines.


  • Ponderous adjectival phrases or clauses

WORDY The targets that are supplied in skeet shooting are discs made of clay

CONCISE Skeet targets are clay discs.


Use Participles (present, past and future)

WORDY It leaves us with the thought that we were hasty.

CONCISE It leaves us thinking that we were hasty.


WORDY This is the idea that was suggested last week.

CONCISE This is the idea suggested last week.


WORDY Because they were tired, the men returned to camp.

CONCISE Tired, the men returned to camp.


Use Predicate Adjectives

  • A predicate adjective stands after the noun it notionally modifies
    • Connected to it by a linking verb (is, are, was, were, seems, becomes …)

The house is large


  • An attributive adjective stands before the noun it modifies

The large house


Predicate adjectives are not necessarily better. But it is better not to restate a word or idea pointlessly

WORDY Riots became frequent affairs

CONCISE Riots became frequent.


WORDY Mr. Martin is a quiet, patient, and cautious person

CONCISE Mr. Martin is quiet, patient, and cautious.


  • Remove empty words and emphasize the adjectives

Do Not State What Sentence Structure Itself Makes Clear

Use Colon or Dash for Announcement

WORDY There were many reasons for the Civil War, which include slavery, economic expansion…

CONCISE There were many reasons for the Civil War: slavery, economic expansion…


WORDY Pitchers are divided into two classes. These classes are starters and relievers.

CONCISE Pitchers are divided into two classes—starters and relievers.


The colon or dash set up an important idea delayed for emphasis:

WORDY But a counterforce has been established within the weapons platoon. This counterforce is the antitank squad.

CONCISE But a counterforce has been established within the weapons platoon—the antitank squad.


Use Ellipses

  • An ellipsis (plural, /ellipses)/is the omission of words implied by the grammar but not necessary to complete the sense. (assumes that readers can supply the missing words from the context.)
  • Place emphasis on brother

WORDY He is taller than his brother is

CONCISE He is taller than his brother.


  • Dropping “lost” from the second clause makes a striking statement
  • Literary flavor, odd in a matter-of-fact, colloquial passage.

WORDY He lost his wallet; she lost her pocketbook.

CONCISE He lost his wallet; she, her pocketbook.


Use Parallelism

WORDYThe beginner must work more slowly, and he must work more consciously.

CONCISE The beginner must work more slowly and more consciously.


  • Governing term need not be stated two or three times
  • But at times parallelism improves nothing
  • Emphasis or rhythm often justifies a certain amount of repetition.
  • Thus the so-called “wordy” version would be preferable if the writer wished to stress “he must work”
